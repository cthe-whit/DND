Character Examples


Here's our character examples

Our method

Here's how we came up with these characters

Where we started

We looked for races and classes that synergized well and took a sample of these base scores: 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18 and assigned them appropriately to keep the characters on even footing. Then we looked for useful skills for these types of characters as well as choosing base equipment where we could and filling in all of the features that the characters come with.

Intentional Blanks

We looked to leave the parts out where possible that you could adjust if you wanted to use these characters and make them your own. We took the liberty of choosing default backgrounds for stat purposes, but otherwise they're ready for you to use! Click on the images to enlarge them and the links below for the full sheets.

Half-Orc Barbarian

Here's our first character




Why Half-Orc

Half-Orcs make good barbarians due to having racial bonuses to both strength and consitution which are core barbarian scores. Additionally they benefit from their racial features of relentless endurance and savage attacks allowing them to fight on the front line more efficiently. Furthermore Half-Orcs and their brutish nature lend themselves to the barbarian class due to their brutish nature in a roleplaying perspective.

Making the barbarian

We looked to assign the ability scores in order of importance being: Strength, Constitution, Dexterity, Wisdom, Charisma, Intelligence. After adding the racial modifiers and features, this leaves us with a character with a lot of strength and hit points to charge in with! Next we chose the skills that we thought were valuable for this class being: Percpetion, Survivial, Athletics, and Intimidation. After that was figured out, we chose base equipment and noted everything given from the outlander backstory as well as the features from the barbarian class itself. Lastly was filling out the bonuses for the skills based on our modifiers and taking into account our proficiencies, noting our hitpoints and calculating our armor class and weapon stats. Elf Ranger

Here's our second character


Why Elf

Elves tend to make good rangers due to having bonuses to dexterity which can be a core skill of the ranger especially if you're looking for an archer style build which we did. The racial features aren't overly beneficial to the Ranger class but are generally helpful all the same. From a roleplaying perspective, Elves and their graceful and seclusive nature fit the role quite well.



Making the Ranger

Firstly, we assigned our ability scores based on importance for the ranger being: Dexterity, Wisdom, Constitution, Charisma, Intelligence, Strength. After adding the Dexterity bonus from the racial modifier and adding the racial features in, we're left with a very dexterous individual that should be quite capable of making some solid ranged shots. Next we chose the skills that we thought were valuable to the class which were: Nature, Stealth, Survival, Athletics. After that, we picked up the base equipment, noted everything from the outlander backstory which we thought suited this class just as well, and added in the features from the ranger class. Lastly, we filled out the bonuses for the skills based on our modifiers and taking our proficiencies into account, noting hitpoints, calculating our armor class and weapon stats. Human Sorcerer

Here's our third character




Why Human

Humans tend to be pretty flexible to just about any class with their racial bonus giving a slight boost to all of their ability scores and the additional language being useful in general. From a roleplaying perspective, humans being a relatively "uninteresting" race on their own are spiced up nicely by adding a class that gets its powers from an event or bloodline of your choosing.

Making the Sorcerer

We assigned ability scores based on importance to the sorcerer being: Charisma, Constitution, Wisdom, Intelligence, Dexterity, Strength. After adding the racial modifiers in, we have a fairly rounded character with a high charisma which is our spellcasting ability, and a high constitution which is used to focus our spells. Next we chose skills that we thought were relevnt to the class which were: Medicine, Religion, Arcane, Insight. Next we chose the base equipment and noted everything from the Hermit backstory as well as the features from the Sorcerer class including the spells we thought fit. Lastly we filled out the bonuses for the skills based on our modifiers and keeping our proficiencies in mind, while calculating our hitpoints, armor class and our weapon and spellcasting stats.
