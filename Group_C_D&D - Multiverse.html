Making a Multiverse

Choose the Environment you would like to build...
Article made by Cameron DeGeorge

Your Multiverse. How is it shaped? I personally like a more cube. So at the center of the Multiverse where maybe the Gods are or somewhere even Gods cannot go for dramatic effect then world kinda branch 4 sectors with little worlds scattered in between at the tips being something special, like a God’s Plane. Yours can be a circle, a square. But Think of the general shape. Not every single world, just general shape. You can expand the worlds as they come up.

The Factions of the Multiverse: Factions of the Multiverse are groups of people who travel the multiverse. I have a few examples. So you can have Warriors of Time and Space in your setting, yours gods or goddesses may not be perfect so they may have made some magical artifacts if in the wrong hands can lead to some great catastrophe. For a few examples, my world, as I explained in my creation myth last time, the Gods of Light and Darkness are at war with each other, so they both of children that help, but they also have two groups they created, The Guardians of Light and The Walkers of Darkness they fight for their respective gods, given God-like powers, sometimes their mortals, sometimes they are demi-gods, but the point being that Gods will tend to interact with the world by different means, it is rare if it is directly. That is what you must think about when creating factions. Nightmare, and Death. If you pop this into Google Translate and set the output language to Latin, you come out with this: deus mortis, tenebris, et sequuntur somnia. Now That is a lot, let’s break it down into the pure core aspects. If you just the three main words, Darkness, Nightmares, and Death you get this: mortem, tenebris, sequuntur somnia. Now when I see this, I like to think, what can I do to forge a name. I split the words. Mor-tene-nia making Mortenenia which sounds like more of a Goddess name, but we are making a God, we need something more menacing. Now I see this Mor-tenebris, Mortenebris sounds good. The key to this is, you have to pick what sounds good, rolls off the tongue, easy to remember. Then alternate from God, Goddess, God, Goddess, until you like the list you make.
