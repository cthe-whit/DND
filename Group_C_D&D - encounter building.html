Challenge ratings

Determining challenge rating is the first step in encounter building


Consider character level

When building an encounter, one of the first steps is to look at the level of the characters in your party. The levels of the characters in your party will help let you determine what challenges they can undertake.





How many monsters

Once you look at the levels of the members of your party, you can decide whether you'd like them to face few stronger monsters or more weaker monsters. The table can be a helpful guide for keeping your encounter balanced so that the fight is challenging enough to be engaging without being too difficult for the players.





Keeping the narrative

The story is just as important to an encounter


Keeping it relevant

It's important when making an encounter to keep it relevant to the story. Keeping enconters relevant helps with keeping the setting and the immersion. The immersion of challenging and relvant encounters can really add to the developement of the story as a whole.



Timing the encounters

Just like how the type of and challenge of an encounter can add to the narrative, the timing can be equally as important. The time that you decide for the party to have their enounter can add a large amount of tension and impact to a story.

Keeping it interesting

Encounters can get dry if you don't spice them up


Interesting monsters

When it can be relevant to the story, choosing an interesting monster can spice up an encounter. A monster can be interesting in appearance and behavior or simply in it's abilities and how it will challenge the players.





Interesting environments

Just like how the monster can keep an encounter fresh, the environment can be an effective means as well. The appearance of the environment that your encounter is in can add to a narrative, such as a throne room or on the edge of a cliff. Interactions with the environment can also add to the encounter. Having still water that makes it more difficult for the party to move in or possibly fighting on unsturdy ground are different ways to help keep players on the edge of their seats.
